Interact with the Map Above!

You may interact with this map and its layers in several different ways.
• First, select an EnviroAtlas community from the list below.
• Next, click on the down arrow of the dropdown menu (at the top right corner of the map) menu to select a baselayer.
• Then, select EnviroAtlas's "Percent Tree Cover" map to view the distribution of trees across the tagret community. Tree cover provides many benefits such as ozone removal (the focus of this web app), air and water filtration, water and carbon storage, natural hazard mitigation, and appealing settings that encourage people to spend time outdoors. Please wait a moment for the map layer to load. In the interim, you can view the factsheet for this layer here.
• Afterward, select the "Acute Respirtatory Symptoms Avoided Due to Ozone Removed by Tree Cover" map layer using the layer control located next to the baselayer dropdown menu. Please wait a moment for the map layer to load. In the interim, you can view the factsheet for this layer here.
• Finally, overlay a 2010 US Census Bureau layer--using the layer control next to the baselayer dropdown--to visually inspect the geographical distribution of the chosen demographic group across the Acute Respirtatory Symptoms Avoided due to Ozone Removed by Tree Cover map layer. Please wait a moment for the map layer to load.
* You may switch to full-screen or zoom into a location of interest using the controls on the left side of the map.

Interact with EnviroAtlas Data Below! Visualizations: Phoenix, Arizona & Tampa, Florida






The information in this section gives an overview of acute respiratory symptoms avoided due to ozone removed by tree cover in Phoenix, Arizona (2010) and Tampa, Florida (2010).

You may interact with the visualizations below by clicking or hovering over a bubble or block group in the map in order to draw deeper inferences about how different populations may be affected by ozone removed by tree cover. The size of each bubble represents the percent of acute respiratory symptoms avoided (within each community or block group) due to ozone removed by tree cover across the entire community. Please note that block groups with high percent tree cover may not have a large population.

Alternatively, explore the tutorial on the left for a step by step guide on using the visualizations below.

After you finish with the visualizations below, please click here or view the final tab of the visualization to explore EnviroAtlas's Eco-Health Relationship Browser to see the science supporting ecosystem services, especially tree cover removal of Ozone!
